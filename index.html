Our Story

A Living Estate
in the Highlands

Nestled near the Machame Gate at the foot of Mount Kilimanjaro, Makoa Farm is a place where time moves differently. The original colonial farmhouse — built in 1901 on a former coffee plantation — has been lovingly preserved and now welcomes guests from around the world.

You are surrounded by the vibrant Chagga community, their coffee farms, and banana groves. To the south, Maasai herders move their cattle across ancient plains. The Makoa and Kikafu river valleys frame the estate, offering gallery forests, hidden waterfalls, and picturesque villages.

Our veterinary practice on the farm operates alongside Tanzanian rangers on wildlife rescue missions. Every booking you make directly funds this work.

Adventures
Around the Farm

From guided river walks to Maasai market visits and big game safaris — there is something extraordinary around every corner of Northern Tanzania.

3–4 Hours

Makoa Nature Walk

Walk across the farm, through bushland, gallery forest, and river valley to hidden spots along the Makoa and Kikafu rivers. Your guide will explain the medicinal uses of native plants along the way.

Swim at a beautiful natural pond, enjoy a waterfall massage, spot primates and exotic birds — and meet Suzie, Lieve, Pina and Fridolin: the farm's resident sheep and donkeys who love to join the walk.

GuidedWildlifeRiverAll Levels
Full Day

Hot Springs Excursion

Depart by 4WD into the Maasai Steppe to reach the legendary Hot Springs south of the farm. A guided walk leads you through a unique biotope between springs and rivers.

Watch for exotic birds, monkeys, shy crocodiles, gazelles, and porcupines. Picnic under ancient fig trees and relax in the warm natural spring waters.

4WDSwimmingWildlifePicnic
Half Day

Maasai Market Visit

Step into one of the most colourful spectacles in Northern Tanzania — a traditional Maasai cattle market. Goods arrive by donkey from the steppe, sometimes after days of travel.

The organised chaos of traders, animals, and barter is an unforgettable window into Maasai life. Optional: combine with a visit to Moshi's old colonial quarter.

CulturalPhotographyLocal Life
Half Day · Fee Applies

Materuni Waterfall

A stunning walking area on the eastern slopes of Kilimanjaro leads to the Materuni Waterfall, with panoramic views through coffee farms and Chagga villages.

The route includes a guided coffee tour: learn how coffee is grown, processed, and roasted — then taste the result fresh from the source.

HikingCoffee TourWaterfall
Full Day · Fees Apply

West Kilimanjaro Big Game

Drive by jeep into the West Kilimanjaro wildlife corridor — a passage connecting Mt Meru, Arusha National Park, and Amboseli in Kenya. Elephants, zebras, wildebeest, eland, and countless antelopes roam freely here alongside Maasai herds.

Optional sunset highlight: a Maasai chief will welcome you into his boma (traditional village) for a private cultural encounter.

Jeep SafariBig 5Maasai BomaSunset
Half Day · Fees Apply

Lake Chala

A spectacular crater lake on the Tanzania–Kenya border, fed by underground springs from Kilimanjaro itself. Its colour shifts from deep blue to turquoise depending on the season, enclosed by a 100-metre crater rim.

Swim in the crystal-clear water, watch baboons play on the rim, and spot endemic Lake Chala Tilapia in the shallows. A short hike reveals the full panorama.

SwimmingBirdingScenic Hike
Full Day · Fees Apply

Kilimanjaro National Park

Day hike in the Kilimanjaro National Park through tropical rainforest — enormous jungle trees, strangler figs, and the legendary black-and-white Colobus monkeys (Guereza) overhead.

Your mountain guide shares deep knowledge of the flora and fauna of Africa's highest peak. A truly immersive experience for nature lovers and keen hikers alike.

Guided HikePrimatesRainforestPark Ranger
Half Day

Chagga Culture & Coffee

A local Chagga guide leads you through his village and reveals the remarkably sophisticated farming system the Chagga have developed over centuries — an ingenious mix of banana groves, coffee plantations, and water management.

Visit a traditional Chagga house, walk the Kikafu stream path, and stop at the Machare Coffee & Tea Farm to taste freshly brewed single-origin Kilimanjaro coffee.

Our Mission

Every Stay
Funds the Wild

Makoa Farm operates a dedicated veterinary practice caring for domestic and wild animals alike. We work in close partnership with Tanzanian rangers on critical wildlife rescue missions across the region.

By choosing Makoa Farm, you become an active supporter of wildlife conservation, environmental preservation, and the wellbeing of the local ecosystem.
